What is Autism?

Autism, also known as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), is a developmental condition that affects how a person thinks, interacts, and experiences the world. People with autism may have challenges in social interaction, communication, and restricted or repetitive behaviors.

Early detection and intervention can improve a child's development and life skills. This tool is designed to help identify possible signs of autism to encourage early support and help parents and caregivers seek professional evaluation if needed.

Common signs of Autism may include:

  • Challenges with communication (e.g., limited speech, difficulty understanding or using language)
  • Difficulty in social interactions (e.g., trouble making eye contact, understanding social cues)
  • Repetitive behaviors (e.g., engaging in the same activities or interests repeatedly)
  • Sensitivity to sensory inputs (e.g., light, sound, touch)

If you notice any of these behaviors, seeking support from a healthcare professional can be helpful in making a more accurate diagnosis.
